Designflow

2.5 days Design Challenge

Challenge

Homely is a concept app created during the Designflow Challenge. This app allows users to rent DIY kits from a nearby location, so they don't have to buy the tools.

Time: 2.5 days

Deliverables:

  • App icon
  • Toolkit Rental screen
  • My Rentals screen
  • How it works screen

Design Process

Research & Analysis

I have analysed the structure of websites and apps that sell DIY tools, in order to understand what kind of patterns users are used to. I have found out what where the most used categories for grouping tools (gardening tools, power tools etc) and noticed the items are often displayed using a grid structure, like in most online shops.

Each item displays an image, title, description, rating and price. The categories buttons have usually an image next to each of them in order to help the user understand which tools are included.

Wireframes

Home screen / Toolkit rental screen

The user can immediately find the needed items from the Popular Toolkits section or from the Categories one.

My Rentals screen

The brief was outlining the need to show both past and current rentals. I decided to add some tabs in order to quickly find the rental needed and reduce cognitive load.

Rent successfully processed & how it works screen

The section explaining in detail the service is shown after an item has been rented. I thought the user would be most interested in learning about all the steps in detail now, after the money transaction.

High fidelity screens

High fidelity screens and the clours and type used.

App Icon

For the app icon I decided to use the symbol of a wrench since it is a well known tool that is also easily recognizable. I picked a brown and yellow palette as I wanted to convey an earthy feeling and the feeling of being in touch with row materials.
